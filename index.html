Mr. Imran Khan

The man who saved Pakistan

Here's a time line of Mr. Imran Khan's life:

  • 1952: Imran Khan was born in Lahore, Pakistan.
  • 1971: He made his cricket debut for Pakistan and went on to become one of the country's most successful cricketers, eventually leading the team to win the 1992 Cricket World Cup.
  • 1982: Imran Khan founded the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital and Research Centre in Lahore, Pakistan, in memory of his mother who had died of cancer. The hospital provides free cancer treatment to underprivileged patients and has become one of the leading cancer hospitals in Pakistan and the region.
  • 1937 - Finishes university and takes a job in the US Forestry Service
  • 1992: Imran Khan led the Pakistani cricket team to victory in the Cricket World Cup, which remains one of the greatest moments in Pakistani sports history.
  • 1996: Imran Khan founded the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) political party, with the aim of creating a corruption-free and just society in Pakistan.
  • 2002: He was elected as a member of the National Assembly of Pakistan for the first time, but his party failed to win a majority in the election.
  • 2013: Imran Khan's PTI party emerged as the second-largest party in the National Assembly after the general election, and he became the leader of the opposition.
  • 2018: After leading a successful election campaign, Imran Khan was elected as the Prime Minister of Pakistan, and his party formed the government.
  • 2019: Imran Khan was honored with the Global Citizenship Award by the Atlantic Council for his efforts to promote peace and democracy in Pakistan and the region.
  • 2020: He was included in Time magazine's list of 100 most influential people in the world.
